Is Mars Really Habitable?

Can humans live on Mars? The Red Planet has water ice, but its thin atmosphere (0.6% of Earth’s pressure) and extreme cold (-80°F average) make survival tough. Radiation is a major risk, with no magnetic field to shield us. NASA’s Perseverance rover found organic molecules, hinting at past life, but terraforming Mars would take centuries. For now, habitats with life support systems are our best bet—think underground bases or 3D-printed domes!
